Double Integrals over Non-Rectangular Regions

We now want to expand our ability to evaluate double integrals over rectangular regions to double integrals over non-rectangular regions. So how can we generalize the process? The following GeoGebra applet will help illustrate how we can compute the volume below a function \(f(x, y)\) over a general region in the \(xy\)-plane. You may also click-and-drag to rotate the view around the origin.

  1. The blue region in the \(xy\)-plane is bounded on by two functions \(g_1(x)\) and \(g_2(x)\) between \(x=0\) and \(x=4\).
  2. We can find the area of the pink region by integrating under \(f\) in a direction parallel to the \(y\)-axis. The only difference from a rectangular region is that we must integrate the pink region between the \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) functions.
  3. Once we have integrated the pink region, we can then integrate over all possible pink regions between \(x=0\) and \(x=4\). This will give us the volume of the solid below \(f\) and over the bounded region.
[https://www.geogebra.org/m/c4q8rf95]

We could follow a similar process if we express \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) as function \(h_1(y)\) and \(h_2(y)\). Then we would first integrate parallel to the \(x\)-axis and again along the \(y\)-axis.

\[\underset{R}{\mathop \iint}\, f(x,y)~dA = \int_{a}^{b} \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)}\, f(x,y)~dy~dx = \int_{c}^{d} \int_{h_1(y)}^{h_2(y)}\, f(x,y)~dx~dy\]
