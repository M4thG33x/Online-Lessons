Divergence Theorem

The Divergence Theorem is an extension of Green's Theorem (in \(\mathbb{R}^2\)) to the divergence of a vector field through a surface in \(\mathbb{R}^3\), just as Stokes' Theorem is an extension of Green's Theorem (in \(\mathbb{R}^2\)) to the curl of a vector field over a surface in \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

Let \(E\) be a simple solid region in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) with a boundary surface \(S\) having a positive (outward) orientation. Let \(\vec{F}\) be a vector field whose components have continuous partial first derivatives on an open region containing \(E\).

\[\underset{S}{\mathop \iint}\, \vec{F} \cdot ~d\vec{S} = \underset{E}{\mathop \iiint}\, \text{div}\, \vec{F} ~dV\]

In other words, the total divergence of a vector field inside a solid region is equal to the divergence of the field across the boundary surface of the solid.
