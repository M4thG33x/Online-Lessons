Divergence

While the curl of a vector field describes its rotation, the divergence of a vector field describes the rate of change inward or outward. Think of divergence describing water in a sink flowing inward towards the drain or flowing outward from the source. A positive divergence would represent an outward flow, like an expanding gas, while a negative divergence would represent in inward flow, like a compressing gas.

The divergence of a vector field \(\vec{F} = \langle P,Q,R \rangle \) on \(ℝ^3\) where \(P\), \(Q\) and \(R\) are differentiable on \(ℝ^3\) is defined by the following formula.

\[\text{div} \, \vec{F} = \nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \frac{\partial P}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial Q}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial R}{\partial z} \]

If \(\nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \vec{0}\), meaning the vector field produces no net expansion or compression,then we say \(\vec{F}\) is source free.

Circulation & Flux

Now that we have discussed curl and divergence, let's revisit Green's Theorem. For a vector field \(\vec{F}\) over a region \(D\) in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) bounded by the curve \(C\), we have already seen from Green's Theorem that the line integral of the tangential component of \(\vec{F}\) along \(C\) is equal to the double integral of the vertical component of the curl of \(\vec{F}\) over \(D\).

Green's Theorem for the circulation of \(\vec{F}\):

\[\underset{C}{\mathop \oint}\, \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ~ds = \underset{D}{\mathop \iint}\, (\nabla \times \vec{F}) \cdot \hat{k}~dA = \underset{D}{\mathop \iint}\, \left( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \right) ~dA\]

We get a similar relationship for the line integral of the normal component of \(\vec{F}\) along \(C\) to the double integral over \(D\) of the divergence of \(\vec{F}\).

Green's Theorem for the flux of \(\vec{F}\):

\[\underset{C}{\mathop \oint}\, \vec{F} \cdot \vec{n}~ds = \underset{D}{\mathop \iint}\, \nabla \cdot \vec{F}~dA = \underset{D}{\mathop \iint}\, \left( \frac{\partial P}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial Q}{\partial y} \right) ~dA\]
