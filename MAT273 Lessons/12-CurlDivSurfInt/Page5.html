Surface Area

Another nice application of these surface parameterizations is the ability to determine the area of a surface. Back in unit 3, we already learned how to compute the area of a surface defined by a function of the form \(z = f(x,y)\). We get a similar result if the surface is defined parametrically.

For a smooth parametric surface \(S\) given by the equation \(\vec{r}(u,v)\) over some region \(D\) (meaning \(u\) and \(v\) are in \(D\)) where \(S\) is covered only once as \(u\) and \(v\) range through \(D\), the surface area of \(S\) over \(D\) is given by the following formula.

\[A = \underset{D}{\mathop \iint}\, \left|\vec{r}_u \times \vec{r}_v\right|~dA\]

If the surface can be defined more explicitly as the function \(z = g(x,y)\) over a region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane, then this simplifies to basically the same formula that we learned back in unit 3.

\[A = \underset{R}{\mathop \iint}\, \sqrt{\left( g_x \right)^2 + \left( g_y \right)^2 + 1}~dA.\]
