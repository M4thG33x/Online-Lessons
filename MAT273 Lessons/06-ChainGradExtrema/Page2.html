Directional Derivatives

In the last lesson, we learned about partial derivatives. Recall that a partial derivative represents the instantaneous rate of change or slope of the tangent line of a surface in a direction that is parallel to the \(x\) or \(y\) axes. We now want to look at a derivative that allows us to compute the slope of the tanganet line in any direction.

The directional derivative is defined as the derivative of a function \(f(x,y)\) at a point \((x_0,y_0)\) in the direction of the unit vector \(\vec{u} = \langle a,b \rangle\) using the following formula.

\[D_{\vec{u}} f(x_0,y_0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_0+ha,y_0+hb)-f(x_0,y_0)}{h}\]

A helpful theorem then let's us rework this formula so that it depends on the partial derivatives. So a more practical formula for the directional derivative of a differentiable function of \(x\) and \(y\) with unit vector \(\vec{u} = \langle a,b \rangle\) is given below.

\[D_{\vec{u}} f(x,y) = f_x(x,y)a + f_y(x,y)b\]

For a surface defined by the function \(z = f(x,y)\), the unit vector \(\vec{u}\) is located in the \(xy\)-plane and \(D_{\vec{u}} f(x,y)\) is the slope of the tangent line in the direction of \(\vec{u}\). Sometimes it is helpful to define \(\vec{u}\) as a vector on the unit circle by \(\vec{u} = \langle \cos(\theta),\sin(\theta) \rangle\).

The following GeoGebra applet will help illustrate the directional derivative for a function \(f(x,y)\). You can use the blue point on the \(xy\)-plane to move the corresponding orange point located on the surface of \(f\). You may also click-and-drag to rotate the view around the origin.

  1. Show the Level Curve in the \(xy\)-plane. Notice that as you move the blue point around, the level curve will change since it depends on the intersection of \(f\) with the horizontal plane \(z=k\), where \(k\) is the height of the orange point on \(f\).
  2. Show the directional derivative Du(a, b). The yellow vector represents the direction of the unit vector u at the blue point \((a,b)\). You can change the direction of u by moving the green point on the \(xy\)-plane.
  3. Show the Tangent line. The slope of the green line is the value of the directional derivative at the orange point, which is above the corresponding blue point \((a,b)\) on the \(xy\)-plane. The line will travel in the direction of u.
