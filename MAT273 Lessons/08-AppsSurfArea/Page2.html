Surface Area

In the previous lesson, we looked at double integrals. One application of double integrals is computing the area of a surface defined by a function of two variables.

For a surface \(z=f(x,y)\) defined with continuous partial derivatives over a region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane, we can compute its area over \(R\) using the formula below.

\[A=\underset{R}{\mathop \iint }\,\sqrt{[f_x(x,y)]^2+[f_y(x,y)]^2+1}~dA\]
